Market Observations

Patterns worth tracking in New Gurgaon's emerging sectors

01

Sector 113 showing accelerated buyer absorption following Dwarka Expressway completion. Inventory levels are tightening with limited new launches entering the corridor

02

SPR corridor demonstrating stronger rental stability relative to appreciation-driven sectors. Consistent end-user demand is underpinning occupancy across Sector 71 and adjacent zones

03

Sectors 93–95 attracting first-time buyer and early-stage investor interest, with one of the few remaining accessible entry points in New Gurgaon's growth belt

Infrastructure Signals

Infrastructure Catalysts

Major infra projects actively reshaping land value and demand across New Gurgaon sectors.

Operational High Impact
Dwarka Expressway
Fully operational 29km expressway connecting Delhi to New Gurgaon, serving as the primary demand driver along its corridor.
Sector 84 Sector 88 Sector 113
Affected Areas
  • Sector 84, Sector 88A, Sector 113, Sector 114
Expected Market Influence
  • Stronger end-user demand along access nodes
  • Increased corridor absorption with fewer unsold units
  • Improved Delhi connectivity driving mid-premium launches
  • Appreciation pressure consolidating near interchange zones
Market Observation
Demand activity appears strongest in sectors with active mid-premium project launches and improving social infrastructure. Corridor is past its early-stage window, with buyer profiles shifting from speculative to end-use.
Operational High Impact
Delhi–Mumbai Expressway (NH-48)
8-lane national highway enabling logistics and industrial growth across the KMP corridor and Manesar belt.
Manesar KMP Corridor
Affected Areas
  • Manesar, IMT Manesar, KMP Corridor fringe sectors
Expected Market Influence
  • Logistics and industrial land demand accelerating
  • Spillover residential interest in adjacent workforce zones
  • Investor attention from industrial and warehousing REIT comparables
  • Employment node proximity influencing buyer profiles
Market Observation
Industrial adjacency is creating a new residential demand dynamic in Manesar. Proximity to employment nodes is shifting buyer mix, and workforce housing demand is increasingly distinct from pure investor demand.
Operational High Impact
KMP Expressway Nodes
Kundli–Manesar–Palwal corridor enabling industrial nodes that underpin the Manesar GIC demand thesis.
Manesar GIC Sector 93–95
Affected Areas
  • Manesar GIC, Sectors 93–95, New Gurgaon fringe
Expected Market Influence
  • Industrial cluster formation driving workforce housing demand
  • Early-stage land value uplift observed in adjacent residential zones
  • Emerging investor attention as the infrastructure playbook resembles early Dwarka Expressway dynamics
Market Observation
Sectors adjacent to KMP nodes are at a comparable infrastructure stage to early Dwarka Expressway development. Early accumulation interest being observed, with a patience horizon of 5 to 7 years for full absorption.
Under Construction Medium Impact
Metro Phase 4 Extension
Gurgaon metro extension targeting SPR and southern New Gurgaon sectors. Expected completion 2027–28.
Sector 71 Sector 82
Affected Areas
  • Sector 71, Sector 82, SPR Corridor mid-zone
Expected Market Influence
  • Mass transit access expected to strengthen end-user demand significantly
  • Mid-segment rental demand likely to improve post-completion
  • Office corridor spillover into adjacent residential activity
  • Anticipatory investor interest building ahead of 2027–28 completion
Market Observation
Metro connectivity consistently improves occupancy and rental stability in suburban corridors. Impact is expected to materialise upon completion. Current activity appears partially anticipatory rather than demand-led.
Planned Medium Impact
DMIC Industrial Hub
Delhi–Mumbai Industrial Corridor node targeting manufacturing and warehousing demand in the New Gurgaon fringe.
Sector 93–95 Manesar
Affected Areas
  • Sectors 93–95, Manesar, New Gurgaon fringe
Expected Market Influence
  • Large-scale manufacturing employment hub expected to drive workforce housing demand
  • Residential land value uplift in sectors within 5–8km of hub zones
  • Investor interest from DMIC adjacency, driven by industrial and REIT demand dynamics
Market Observation
DMIC designation typically precedes 5–8 year demand cycles in adjacent residential markets. Early observation stage currently. This remains a speculative but infrastructure-backed thesis for patient capital.
